The Impact of Social Media on Society

Definition

Social media is a computer-based technology that facilitates the sharing of ideas, thoughts, and information through the building of virtual networks and communities. By design, social media is Internet-based and gives users quick electronic communication of content. Content includes personal information, documents, videos, and photos. Users engage with social media via a computer, tablet, or smartphone via web-based software or applications.

Choices:

1. Data, Choosing which information to collect. Presenting personal data to customers. (Photos)

2. Targeted Advertising, Deciding which activity to track. Used to provide ads that are personalized.

3. Free Communication, Free choice of consumers to decide which media platform to communicate with (e.g. Messenger)


Opportunities:

1. In business, the enterprise can target and appeal to consumers easily through effective and efficient marketing.

2. In individual, they have more job opportunities through social media and job seekers can be easy to find a job through websites such as a LinkedIn.

3. In education, schools can prevent being in a bad situation and students can access lectures online.


Risks:

1. Privacy, Most social media apps track your phone, such as what you search and look at and give relative adds.

2. Productivity Loss, Some companies have been losing money due to workers productivity loss from using social media apps.

3. Vigilantism, Social media platforms taking matters into their own hands to be judge, jury, and executioner.
